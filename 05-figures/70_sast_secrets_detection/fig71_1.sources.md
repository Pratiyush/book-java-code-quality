# Source-trace sidecar — Fig 71.1
## Secrets detection: a ladder of catches, cheapest first — and why finding is already too late

Chapter 31 · keys 70 + 71 (owner of this figure: 71) · "Catching What You Forgot" · Part VIII
Family: Flow-sequence (defense-in-depth ladder along the commit→remote lifecycle)
Placement: §"Secrets detection: and why finding is too late" — at the CONCEPT block
"A ladder of catches, cheapest first," before the found=compromised=rotate paragraph
One idea: Secrets detection is defense in depth across the lifecycle; each later stage backstops the
one before because the earlier ones are bypassable, but only the pre-commit stage actually prevents
the leak. Past the remote the secret is compromised — the only fix is to rotate it; deleting from
HEAD or even rewriting history is necessary, not sufficient.

This figure is the one specified in the key 71 dossier §6: "Fig 71.1 — defense in depth:
pre-commit → push protection → CI → history scan, with 'found = rotate' at the end."
