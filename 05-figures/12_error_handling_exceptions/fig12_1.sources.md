**Figure:** Fig 10.1 — The Throwable hierarchy and the checked/unchecked decision  
**Chapter:** 10 (dossier key 12) — When Things Go Wrong  
**Family:** Architecture / concept-map  
**One idea:** Java's Throwable hierarchy has exactly three meaningful kinds; the checked/unchecked split is enforced at compile time by the catch-or-specify rule (JLS §11.2), and Effective Java Item 70 gives the decision rule: recoverable→checked, programming error→unchecked, JVM error→Error (do not throw or subclass).

## Accessibility

- Alt-text: Throwable hierarchy tree (Exception, RuntimeException, Error) above a three-card Item 70 checked/unchecked/Error decision rule.
- Long-description: A diagram titled "Fig 10.1 — The Throwable hierarchy and the checked/unchecked decision," subtitled "JLS SE 21 §11 hierarchy · §11.2 catch-or-specify rule · Effective Java 3e Item 70 decision." The top half is a tree. A root node, "Throwable (java.lang — JLS SE 21 §11)," branches down to three sibling nodes left to right. The left node, "Exception (minus RuntimeException)," carries the badge "compile-time gate (catch-or-specify, JLS §11.2)" and has two sub-nodes, IOException and SQLException. The middle node, "RuntimeException (unchecked subtype of Exception)," carries the badge "no compile-time requirement" and has two sub-nodes, IllegalArgumentException and NullPointerException. The right node, "Error (JVM's — do not throw or subclass, except AssertionError)," carries the badge "unchecked · JVM-reserved" and has two sub-nodes, OutOfMemoryError and AssertionError. The bottom half is a panel titled "Effective Java 3e, Item 70 — decision rule (one sentence per kind)" holding three cards. The first card, "Checked (Exception − RuntimeException)," reads: use for conditions the caller can reasonably recover from, such as a file that may not exist or a remote call that may time out; enforced by Checkstyle IllegalThrows and Sonar java:S112. The second card, "Unchecked (RuntimeException)," reads: use for programming errors, broken preconditions, "When in doubt, throw unchecked" (Item 70); enforced by PMD AvoidThrowingRawExceptionTypes and Sonar java:S112. The third card, "Error," reads: reserved for the JVM (resource exhaustion, unrecoverable state), do not throw or subclass except AssertionError, with the Item 70 quote "you shouldn't throw them either, and you should not subclass them." A note bar below states that the failure model is a contract: declared checked exceptions are API and callers plan recovery around them, unchecked exceptions signal bugs to debug, and catching too broadly (catch (Exception e) {}) violates Items 70 and 77 and is flagged by Checkstyle IllegalCatch, PMD EmptyCatchBlock, and SpotBugs DE_MIGHT_IGNORE. A source line closes the figure.
- Grayscale/contrast/legibility note: Grayscale-safe. The three tree branches differ by a tint lightness ramp (Exception darker, RuntimeException light, Error darkest) and, decisively, by their type names, position, and badge text, never by hue alone. Each gate badge states its rule in words ("compile-time gate…", "no compile-time requirement", "unchecked · JVM-reserved"), so the green/amber/red badge tints are redundant. The three decision cards are titled "Checked"/"Unchecked"/"Error" and ordered left to right, so their colored left borders are not load-bearing. Dark text on light tints reads at print size; all type names, rule IDs, and the catch snippet are authored selectable text, understandable without syntax color.
