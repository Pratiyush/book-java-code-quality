## Accessibility

- Alt-text: Qualitative line chart of cost per feature over time: a high-internal-quality line stays low while a low-internal-quality line rises steeply.
- Long-description: A line chart with two diverging curves. The vertical axis is "Cost per feature", labelled low, med, high from bottom to top; the horizontal axis is "Time", running from "Start" at the left to "Later" at the right. Both curves begin together at the same low point on the left, marked "both start here". A thin solid line, labelled "High internal quality (cruft stays low)", stays nearly flat and low across the whole chart. A thicker dashed line, labelled "Low internal quality (cruft accumulates)", climbs gently at first and then accelerates upward toward "high" on the right. A mid-chart annotation, "cruft tax widens", points to the growing vertical gap between the two lines. The axes carry no numeric values; the note states the chart is a qualitative illustration and that the shape and the definition of "cruft" come from Fowler — cruft being "the difference between the current code and how it would ideally be".
- Grayscale-safe: Yes. The two curves are distinguished by line style (thin solid vs thick dashed) and by their end labels, not by hue. Reads in black-and-white print.
- Contrast: Sufficient. Black curves on white plot area; axis and label text dark on white.
- Code legibility: Not applicable — the figure carries no code listing.
