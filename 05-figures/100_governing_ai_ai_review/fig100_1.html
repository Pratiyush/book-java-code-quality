Where AI assists · where deterministic gates decide · where the human gate is the invariant

Author

Developer authors code — AI assistant as co-pilot (sanctioned tool)

Tool selection = a security & compliance decision: vet for data-handling (does code leave the org?), license / IP status of suggestions, privacy posture. Disclosure & provenance: record where AI was used. Ch 42 (key 100)

AI drafts; developer owns.

AI review
(augment only)
AI code reviewer — PR bot comments on the diff
Scoped to the diff; given team standards as context (Ch 37, key 86). Finds some bugs in natural language; surfaces missing tests / edge cases. Drafts findings — a human dispositions every comment.
Ceiling: cannot verify intent — infers from code, no ground truth of what code should do. AI reviewing AI compounds blind spots; independence (different model / human) is the safeguard. (Ch 42, key 98)
Augments; never replaces the gate.
No auto-merge on AI approval.
↓
Human
gate

Human reviewer — intent verification (the invariant)

Reviews the diff for correctness of intent: is this what the system is actually supposed to do? Intent lives outside the code — in requirements, business rules, user need. Only a human author has it; only a human reviewer can challenge it.

No auto-merge on AI approval Accountability stays with the author Ch 37 (key 84) · Ch 42 (key 100)
The one job that is structurally human — AI changes how drafts are written, not who answers for the result.
↓
Ship

Merge — only with all gates green and human approval

Counter-metric the outcome: track productivity and change-failure rate. AI can write code; only policy can ship it. Ch 38 (key 85) · Ch 42 (key 100)

Measure both sides: productivity gain and risk signal.
Policy governs tool selection (a security decision) and mandates disclosure and provenance. Culture sustains it: shadow AI — unsanctioned tools leaking code — is the failure mode of prohibition and of policy without culture. Ch 42 (key 100) · Ch 1 (key 06)
AI review patterns: scope to the diff; give it your standards checklist as context (Ch 37, key 86); ask for specific lenses (security, concurrency, error-handling); AI drafts findings, human dispositions — never auto-merge on AI approval. When AI generated the code, ensure an independent reviewer (human and/or different-model tooling): correlated blind spots compound, they do not cancel. Ch 42 (key 98)
