Figure 37.1 — The review-effectiveness curve: small and fast, or it does not work

Defect detection holds in a small effective zone and collapses past it as reviewer fatigue sets in — figures are the Cohen / SmartBear study's.

Inside the zone — review works
Roughly 100–300 lines over ~30–60 minutes of attention. The reviewer can hold the change in their head, so design and logic flaws get caught. Small pull requests are a precondition for review, not a nicety — which is why trunk-based development (Chapter 35) and effective review reinforce each other.
Past the zone — review skims
Beyond roughly 300–400 lines, defect detection drops sharply as reviewer fatigue sets in. A “LGTM” on a 2,000-line diff is theater: the act of approval without the substance of review — the same presence-without-quality trap a green automated gate can hide.

Effective review zone (~100–300 LOC, ~30–60 min) and the drop in defect detection past it: Jason Cohen / SmartBear, Best Kept Secrets of Peer Code Review — the figures are the study's, attributed. The curve shape is qualitative (the study reports detection drops past the zone); no defect-rate value is invented.
