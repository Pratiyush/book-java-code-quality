## Accessibility
- Alt-text: A line chart of defects caught per review against pull-request size, peaking in a small effective zone then collapsing past it.
- Long-description: The horizontal axis is lines changed per pull request (ticks at 0, 100, 300, ~400, 1,000+, and 2,400); the vertical axis is defects caught per review, from low to high, with no numeric defect-rate value. A shaded band from 100 to 300 lines is labelled "Effective zone, ~100–300 LOC, ~30–60 min"; a lighter shaded region from ~400 lines rightward is labelled "Detection drops, reviewer fatigue." The curve rises steeply from low at 0 lines to a high plateau across the effective zone, then falls steadily through the fatigue region toward low at the far right. An accent dot at 2,400 lines, joined by a dashed leader, is labelled "2,400-line PR = 'LGTM' in 4 min, theater." Two cards below restate the reading: inside the zone (~100–300 lines, ~30–60 minutes) review catches design and logic flaws and small pull requests are a precondition reinforced by trunk-based development (Chapter 35); past ~300–400 lines review skims and a "LGTM" on a 2,000-line diff is theater — presence without quality, the same trap a green automated gate can hide. The figures are attributed to the Cohen / SmartBear study.
- Grayscale / contrast / legibility: Reads in grayscale. The curve is a single dark line read by its shape (rise into a plateau, then a fall), and the two regions are distinguished by position and by their labels — the effective zone is a mid-grey shaded band labeled "~100–300 LOC, ~30–60 min" and the fatigue region is a lighter band labeled "Detection drops, reviewer fatigue" — not by hue. The far-right 2,400-line point uses the one warm accent but is also a marked dot with a dashed leader and a text label ("LGTM in 4 min, theater"), and both axes are titled with worded ticks. Dark ink on light fills holds ample contrast; the figure carries no code listing, and no distinction depends on color alone.
