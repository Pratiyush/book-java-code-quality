## Accessibility
- Alt-text: A three-by-three matrix mapping three license-obligation tiers (permissive, weak copyleft, strong copyleft) against three distribution modes (internal-only, SaaS, shipped binary), showing each cell's risk as Low, Review, or High, above a "not legal advice" caution.
- Long-description: A four-step strip across the top reads Identify (normalize to an SPDX identifier, read off the SBOM), Categorize (by obligation), Gate (an allow/deny policy via license-maven-plugin, FOSSA, or ScanCode that fails the build), and Attribute (auto-generate the THIRD-PARTY/NOTICE file). Below it a matrix has three obligation-tier rows and three distribution-mode columns. Permissive (Apache-2.0, MIT, BSD; attribution only) is Low risk in all three modes. Weak copyleft (LGPL-2.1, MPL, EPL; file- or library-level share-alike) is Low when internal-only and Review for SaaS and shipped. Strong copyleft (GPL-3.0-only, AGPL; derivative-work obligations) is Review when internal (GPL is low if never distributed, but AGPL reaches network use), and High for both SaaS (AGPL reaches the deployment) and shipped (derivative-work obligation on a proprietary product). Risk is shown by cell fill weight and the explicit word in each cell, not by color alone. A legend defines Low, Review, and High. Three notes follow: this is not legal advice (tools detect declared licenses only); detection is imperfect (verify high-risk findings by hand); and the transitive surprise plus license-is-not-security caveat (scan the full graph; a license-clean dependency can still be vulnerable).
