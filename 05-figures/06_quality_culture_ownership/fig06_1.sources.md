## Accessibility

- Alt-text: Three-column comparison of Westrum's pathological, bureaucratic, and generative cultures across information flow and response to failure; generative is highlighted.
- Long-description: A three-column comparison, one column per culture type, each compared across five rows: information flow, cooperation level, cross-team bridging, response to failure, and new ideas. The left column, "Pathological — power-oriented": information is hoarded and shared only when it serves the holder's power; cooperation is low; cross-team bridging is discouraged; the response to failure is blame and scapegoating with the messenger punished; new ideas are crushed if they threaten existing power. The middle column, "Bureaucratic — rule-oriented": information is channelled through silos and procedures; cooperation is modest; bridging is tolerated but needs formal approval; the response to failure is justice sought and rule adherence investigated; new ideas create problems if they threaten existing process. The right column, "Generative — performance-oriented", is the highlighted focus column (heavier border and an outline): information is actively sought and good and bad news shared freely; cooperation is high; bridging is actively encouraged; the response to failure is inquiry not blame, surfacing systemic causes; new ideas are implemented as conscious inquiry. A callout in the generative column states the DORA finding that generative culture is associated with improved software-delivery performance and that psychological safety, the generative hallmark, was found predictive of delivery performance, organizational performance, and productivity.
- Grayscale-safe: Yes, with a noted reinforcement. The generative column is the focus by border weight, an outline, the "Performance-oriented" subhead, its rightmost position, and the DORA callout — not by hue. The "response to failure" row sets its three values in red, amber, and green respectively, but each value carries fully distinct wording, so the distinction survives in grayscale; the color is reinforcement, not the sole carrier.
- Contrast: Sufficient. Dark ink on light column fills; the colored failure-response text is dark enough to read in grayscale.
- Code legibility: Not applicable — the figure carries no code listing.
