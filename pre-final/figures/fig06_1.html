Fig 06.1 — Westrum's three organizational culture types

Information flow and response to failure distinguish the types; DORA associates the generative type with improved software-delivery performance.

Pathological Power-oriented
Information flow
Hoarded; shared only when it serves the holder's power
Cooperation level
Low; personal survival over team goals
Cross-team bridging
Discouraged; turf protection is the norm
Response to failure
Blame and scapegoating; messenger punished
New ideas
Crushed if they threaten existing power
Bureaucratic Rule-oriented
Information flow
Channelled through established silos and procedures
Cooperation level
Modest; departments protect their turf
Cross-team bridging
Tolerated but requires formal approval paths
Response to failure
Justice sought; rule adherence investigated
New ideas
Creates problems if they threaten existing process
Generative Performance-oriented
Information flow
Actively sought; good news and bad news shared freely
Cooperation level
High; team mission over personal status
Cross-team bridging
Actively encouraged; knowledge flows across boundaries
Response to failure
Inquiry, not blame; failure surfaces systemic causes
New ideas
Implemented; conscious inquiry is the norm
DORA finding: generative culture is associated with improved software-delivery performance (DORA four keys) and organizational outcomes. Psychological safety — the generative hallmark — was found predictive of delivery performance, organizational performance, and productivity.
Source: dora.dev / 2019 State of DevOps; Accelerate (2018)

Westrum typology (Ron Westrum, 1988) as described in Accelerate (Forsgren/Humble/Kim, 2018) and the DORA generative organizational culture capability page (dora.dev).
