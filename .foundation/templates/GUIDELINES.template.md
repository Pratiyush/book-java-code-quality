## 0. What this book is

A comprehensive, authoritative guide to **{{BOOK_SUBJECT}}**, written for readers who want to understand **how it works, why it works that way, and when (and when not) it applies**.

**ONE book (locked).** The entries in `01-index/CANDIDATE_POOL.md` are a **candidate pool**, not the table of contents. The keys in that pool are frozen and double as the dossier key for every artifact (`NN_slug`). The book of record is the culled, reshuffled `01-index/FINAL_INDEX.md` (see that file for the canonical count), which gates all drafting. Per-chapter status across every gate lives in `01-index/CHAPTER-TRACKER.md`.

The work runs in phases: research the pool → score for inclusion and cull to ONE volume → draft the selected chapters → assemble. Drafting a chapter is hard-blocked until `FINAL_INDEX.md` is human-confirmed.

## 1. The core principle

**One chapter at a time. Depth over speed.** Each chapter gets its own research pass and its own writing pass, runs the full gated pipeline, and is reviewed and human-approved before the next is approved. Research may run ahead in parallel into a dossier bank; only drafting-to-approval is strictly sequential.

---

## 2. The seven HARD rules (non-negotiable content floors)

These are absolute. A chapter that violates any one of them does not ship — no exceptions, no override by score.

1. **Neutrality by the stance for this book type.** {{NEUTRALITY_STANCE}} — for {{BOOK_SUBJECT}} this is *{{BOOK_SUBJECT}} is the subject; competing/alternative subjects appear **only** for a necessary direct comparison or a migration/translation topic, and the book never crowns any of them superior. Banned phrasings include "better than", "unlike X", "the problem with X", "superior", and "beats". Any cross-subject claim must carry a cited source.* Full law and the allowed/forbidden phrasing table: `NEUTRALITY.md`.

2. **Honest limitations — no winner.** Every feature/claim gets its strongest case **and** its hardest objections, including an explicit "when NOT to use / when this does not hold". Nothing is sold; nothing is mocked. (Absorbs fair-presentation and balance/tone.)

3. **Never invent a fact.** Never invent {{INVENT_UNITS}}. Every specific fact traces to a citation against the pinned authority. Untraceable detail is cut, or marked `UNVERIFIED` and flagged to `09-flags/`.

4. **Quoted/displayed material is bounded and verified.** Each {{EXAMPLE_POLICY}} unit is capped at the per-snippet limit (≤ 9 lines for code; the equivalent bounded extract for a quote), and every displayed unit is verified against the pinned source/docs, recording the source path. (Fair use; see `LEGAL-IP-RULES.md`.) *(Technical profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md: the one capstone module is the SINGLE sanctioned exception — it may show full-file listings over the line cap and cross-module wiring, and is gated like any companion module ({{BUILD_CMD}} green + CODE-REVIEW). No other module or chapter carries this exception. Book types with {{GATES_OFF}} including example-build drop the capstone-module clause.)*

5. **Authenticity.** Every chapter is AI-produced. The AUDIT gate reads the chapter cold and must judge that a sharp reader cannot tell a machine wrote it. PASS or it loops back.

6. **Figures are load-bearing but no longer rare.** A chapter carries a per-chapter image budget (§8) per {{FIGURE_POLICY}}: typically one to two designed conceptual diagrams plus zero to a few captured native screenshots/figures; **zero figures is now the exception**, reserved for short or pure-reference chapters. Every figure is load-bearing, never quota-filling. **Designed diagrams are authored as HTML and rendered to a cropped screenshot — never generated by an image model** (authored HTML keeps every label exact and source-traced; see §8). Tables are **one** visual-rhythm lever among several (figures, listings, lists, callouts, ASCII sketches), not the primary one. *(Technical/operational profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md: third-party-tool screenshots are **not used**; a designed topology/flow diagram takes their place. Non-screenshot book types substitute rights-cleared photos/maps or data figures per their profile.)*

7. **One locked voice** — the narrative defined in `VOICE-GUIDE.md`: {{VOICE}}. Drift from it fails the AUDIT gate.

## 3. The authority pin (HARD)

Every {{INVENT_UNITS}} unit and every displayed extract traces to **{{AUTHORITY_SOURCE}}** at the pin **{{AUTHORITY_PIN}}** — **never** to a moving/unreleased target (e.g. a `main`/`SNAPSHOT` branch or an unpinned later edition), and **never** to a fast-moving non-stable source. The pin, the exact identifier, the local clone/fetch path (`{{AUTHORITY_CLONE_PATH}}`), and the re-evaluation policy for the next pin live in **`SOURCE-PIN.md`**, which the deterministic source-pin check enforces.

## 4. Research

Before a word is drafted, the topic gets a structured research pass into a dossier (see `templates/RESEARCH-TEMPLATE.md`), surveying the pinned authority and supporting sources. Each dossier establishes: the core definition and origin, the mechanism (how the thing works), the evidence for it (working examples/cases), the evidence against it and its honest "when NOT to use / when this does not hold" limits, its current status and version/edition compatibility, and gap-filling for any detail still uncertain.

**Dossier quality is measured by source count, verified extracts, and filled sections — never by word count.** Go as deep as the topic demands; do not pad.

---

## 5. Citations & sources (house style)

- Every specific fact traces to a citation.
- **In the text:** name the source inline ("the {{AUTHORITY_SOURCE}} states…", "the … guide describes…").
- **End of every chapter:** a "Sources and further reading" section in **two tiers** — *Primary / official* (the pinned authority, source, release notes, official channels) and *Accessible / further reading* (tutorials, talks, community writing). Plain-text URLs; no hidden hyperlinks.
- **Never invent a source.** A source that cannot be verified is flagged to `09-flags/`.

**Source ranking (highest to lowest):** the pinned authority docs → the pinned source / reference → governing specs & standards → release notes / official channel → reputable secondary writing / talks → casual Q&A (color only, never sole support). **Never** use content farms or AI-generated text as a factual source. This is house style — pick the citation model (house, or academic author-year/DOI) that matches your book type and state it here.

## 6. Voice & chapter structure

Voice is delegated in full to **`VOICE-GUIDE.md`**: {{VOICE}} — invisible narrator, each term glossed once, heavy on worked material, uncertainty stated openly and once. Do not re-specify voice here.

Chapter structure follows **`templates/CHAPTER-TEMPLATE.md`**: title → subtitle (topic + index # + Part) → narrative body (hook → overview → how it works → deep dive → limitations → when *not* to use → alternatives → hand-off) → back matter (key concepts, reference, sources) → next-chapter teaser. An epigraph is optional, never required. Length is variable by substance — **never pad to hit a length, and there are no per-chapter word-count floors.**

## 7. The pipeline

The full, implementable, gated flow is defined in **`PIPELINE.md`** and is the authority on step order. In short: pin the source → research into a dossier → source-trace audit (HARD) → inclusion score + cull (HARD) → draft from the dossier only → *(technical profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md)* **Step 4b EXAMPLE-BUILD (HARD)** — build one self-contained runnable enterprise-grade module per chapter and confirm it compiles green at the pin via `{{BUILD_CMD}}` *(book types with {{GATES_OFF}} including example-build skip this step and use citation-verify in its place)* → VERIFY on the draft against the source/compiled material (HARD) → clarity review (HARD) → AUDIT for neutrality, voice, and authenticity (HARD) → chapter scorecard (HARD) → figures: design each planned diagram as HTML, render to a cropped PNG (HARD for any planned figure) → cross-chapter reconciliation (HARD) → maintainer update + learning capture → **STOP at the human approval gate (HARD)** → approve, finalize, commit → assemble.

**Scoring** is delegated entirely to **`SCORING.md`**: five clusters scored 1–10 — {{SCORING_CLUSTERS}} — plus the content-floors that must pass: {{FLOORS}}. A chapter ships only if all applicable floors pass and the rubric clears {{SHIP_BAR}}. *(Technical profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md: FLOOR C is **SOURCE-TRACE / COMPILE / CODE-REVIEW** — zero invented {{INVENT_UNITS}}, the chapter's companion module compiles green at the pin via `{{BUILD_CMD}}`, and the module passes the CODE-REVIEW gate. Book types with {{GATES_OFF}} keep FLOOR C as source-trace only, dropping the compile + code-review clauses.)* Do not invent alternate rubrics here.

**Parallel batches.** When drafting or researching several chapters at once, agree a hand-off contract up front (what each chapter OWNS / must not touch, exact forward-hook targets, shared canonical facts — the {{INVENT_UNITS}} that recur) and run cross-chapter reconciliation against `LEDGER.md` afterward to catch continuity drift, broken hand-offs, and duplicated extracts.

**Read in full — no RAG, no excerpting (HARD).** Every agent reads its inputs whole: the entire dossier, the entire draft, the relevant neighbor chapters, and the full law docs. Never substitute retrieval or chunk-sampling for a quality-critical read. Manage the context limit by committing per batch and resuming after reset — never by reading less.

## 8. Figures & visual rhythm

Figures are **load-bearing but no longer rare.** Every chapter carries an image budget, sized to its class, per {{FIGURE_POLICY}}. A real comparable book carries its visual load through **figures and listings**, not tables (a comparable title runs ~3 tables in the whole book). Zero figures is the **exception**, reserved for short or pure-reference chapters.

**Per-chapter image budget (class-aware).** Tie the budget to the chapter's class. *(The class rows below are the technical-profile shape — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md; relabel the classes and the captured-screenshot column for your book type, but keep the budget-by-class structure and the "zero figures permitted" carve-out for short/pure-reference chapters.)*

| Chapter class | Designed diagrams (HTML → render) | Captured native screenshots/figures | Inline ASCII | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Internals** (deep mechanism chapters) | 1–2 | 0–1 | 2–4 | 3–5 |
| **Operational** (hands-on / tooling chapters) | 1 | 2–4 | 1–3 | 5–8 |
| **Flow / process** (sequence- or state-heavy chapters) | 1–2 | 1–2 | 1–2 | 4–6 |
| **Pure-reference / short** | 0–1 | 0–1 | 2–3 | 2–4 (zero figures permitted) |

The chapter's **figure plan** (which diagrams, which screenshots) is fixed at draft time (Step 4), designed and rendered at Step 9, and reviewed by the human **with the chapter** at Step 12. See `PIPELINE.md`.

**When-to-use test (per figure).** Each figure earns its place only if it carries a spatial/flow/architecture model, a comparison, or a real surface that prose handles poorly. A figure is never added to hit the budget; an ASCII sketch is preferred for a quick ordered flow.

**Diagram families** (all designed via the HTML method below): architecture/layering · flow/sequence · comparison/benchmark · state/lifecycle · decision · topology/deployment · data/domain-model. The eighth is the **captured** family per {{FIGURE_POLICY}} (native UI screenshots for a technical book; rights-cleared photos/maps or data figures for other types).

**Mandatory visual-rhythm floor (READABILITY / AUDIT criterion).** No wall of grey text or back-to-back displayed material. Every chapter carries visual texture through several levers — and **must not lean on tables alone** (the comparable genre uses ~3 tables in the whole book):

- **Figures** — designed diagrams and captured screenshots/figures, per the budget above. The primary visual anchor of a page.
- **Listings** — the worked extracts (≤9 lines for code, the equivalent bounded extract otherwise), introduced in prose and referenced by what they show. *(Technical profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md: the one capstone module is the SINGLE sanctioned exception — it may show full-file listings over the cap and cross-module wiring; it is gated like any companion module ({{BUILD_CMD}} green + CODE-REVIEW). No other module or chapter carries this exception.)*
- **Tables** — trade-off, when-NOT-to-use, and decision tables. One lever among several; pure-reference tables (full dumps, long lists) belong in back matter, not the reading body.
- **Bullet lists** — for enumerable points, options, and steps.
- **Callouts** — sparing, from the fixed named taxonomy NOTE / TIP / IMPORTANT / CONCEPT / WARNING / TRY IT (defined in `templates/CHAPTER-TEMPLATE.md`); never stacked, never directly under a heading.
- **Fenced ASCII sketches** — sequence, flow, or ordering diagrams for anything that happens in an order. These live inline and need no render step.

**How a designed figure is produced (the HTML method — HARD).** Designed diagrams are **authored as HTML, rendered by headless Chrome, and cropped to a PNG** — never generated by an image model.

- **HTML is the source of truth.** Each diagram is a self-contained HTML file at `05-figures/NN_slug/figNN_x.html`, styled by the shared house stylesheet `05-figures/_assets/figures.css` (one palette, grayscale-safe, print DPI). Shapes are HTML/CSS + inline SVG.
- **Text is baked in — deliberately.** Because the HTML is authored (not generated), every label, arrow, and number is exact and **source-traced**. The labels live in the image; the caption still carries the one-sentence idea.
- **Render + crop.** `node 05-figures/_assets/render.mjs <fig.html> <fig.png> "#figure" 3` screenshots the `#figure` element at 3× for print. Both the HTML and the PNG are committed.
- **Figure-accuracy gate.** A sidecar `figNN_x.sources.md` traces every depicted claim to the **same pinned {{AUTHORITY_PIN}} paths** as the prose. Comparison/benchmark numbers use pinned-source figures only; where the source gives none, bars are sized relatively with no numeric axis. Nothing is depicted that the prose has not traced.
- **Neutral imagery** (no vendor logos/branding). *(Technical/operational profile — see BOOK-TYPE-PROFILES.md: third-party-tool screenshots are never used — a designed topology/flow diagram replaces them.)*
- **Captured native UIs/figures** are screenshot from the running pinned material (Step 4c), saved with a source+command+URL manifest as their source trace.
- **Consistent house style** across all chapters via the one stylesheet. See `templates/FIGURE-GUIDE.md`.

## 11. Continuous improvement (HARD RULE)

After every meaningful unit of work — a chapter researched, a draft, an audit, a blocked or failed run, a workaround:

- **Capture learnings as you go** — append to `PIPELINE-LEARNINGS.md`.
- **Always close with suggestions** — every report ends with a short "Learnings & pipeline suggestions" note.
- **Roll proven lessons into the rules** — promote a confirmed learning into the relevant file (this file, a companion, a template, an agent spec, or a script).
- **The book-maintainer records it** — each `LEDGER.md` update logs the new learning and whether it was promoted.

Use `/retro` to formalize this at phase boundaries.
